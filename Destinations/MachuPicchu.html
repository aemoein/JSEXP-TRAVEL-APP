Michu Picchu

Machu Picchu is a stunning Incan citadel located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a popular tourist destination and offers a glimpse into the ancient civilization of the Incas. Visitors can explore the ruins of the city, hike through the surrounding mountains, or simply take in the breathtaking views of the valley below. Machu Picchu is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure.

Quick Facts

Location: Andes Mountains, Peru
Founded: 15th century
Altitude: 2,430 meters (7,970 feet)
Designation: UNESCO World Heritage Site
Access: Train or hiking
Best Time to Visit: May to September

Climate

Machu Picchu has a subtropical highland climate with two distinct seasons: a dry season from May to September and a wet season from October to April. The best time to visit is during the dry season when temperatures are mild and the skies are clear.

Top Attractions

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is one of the most famous archaeological sites in the world. Located in the Andes Mountains of Peru, this ancient city was built by the Inca Empire in the 15th century and features stunning stone structures and terraces.

Huayna Picchu

This mountain peak located above Machu Picchu offers breathtaking views of the ancient ruins and surrounding mountains. Visitors can climb to the top via a steep trail for an unforgettable experience.

Inca Bridge

This unique structure is a narrow stone walkway built into the side of a cliff. Originally used by the Inca people as a secret entrance to Machu Picchu, the Inca Bridge offers stunning views and a thrilling experience for visitors.

Temple of the Sun

This impressive structure is one of the most well-preserved buildings in Machu Picchu. It was likely used for religious and ceremonial purposes by the Inca people and features stunning stone carvings and intricate design.

Food & Drink

Sushi

Vancouver is known for its fresh seafood and sushi is a popular choice for locals and visitors alike. Some top spots to try include Miku, Tojo's, and Sushi Bar Maumi.

Craft Beer

Vancouver is also home to a thriving craft beer scene, with a variety of local breweries and taprooms. Some popular options include Brassneck Brewery, Main Street Brewing, and Strange Fellows Brewing.

Poutine

This quintessential Canadian dish consists of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy. Some top spots to try include Fritz European Fry House, La Belle Patate, and Smoke's Poutinerie.

Hotels & Accommodation

Fairmont Pacific Rim

This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of downtown Vancouver and offers stunning views of the harbour and mountains. Amenities include a rooftop pool, spa, and multiple dining options.

Loden Hotel

This boutique hotel is situated in the trendy Yaletown neighbourhood and offers stylish rooms and suites with high-end amenities. Guests can also enjoy the on-site fitness centre and spa.

The Burrard

This retro-style hotel is located in the vibrant neighbourhood of downtown Vancouver and offers quirky rooms with mid-century modern decor. Amenities include a courtyard and bike rentals.

Getting Around

SkyTrain

The SkyTrain is Vancouver's rapid transit system, providing quick and convenient access to downtown, the airport, and other key destinations. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

Bike Share

Vancouver also has a bike share program, with hundreds of bikes available for rent at various stations throughout the city. Users can sign up for a membership or purchase a day pass.

Ferry

For those looking to explore the surrounding areas, ferries are available from Vancouver to destinations like Victoria, the Gulf Islands, and the Sunshine Coast. Reservations are recommended, especially during peak season.
