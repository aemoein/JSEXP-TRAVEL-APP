Iceland

Iceland is a beautiful island nation located in the North Atlantic. Known for its breathtaking natural landscapes, including glaciers, geysers, and hot springs, Iceland is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The country is also steeped in history and folklore, with a rich cultural heritage that includes traditional music, dance, and literature. Visitors can explore the capital city of Reykjavik, with its vibrant nightlife and unique architecture, or venture out into the countryside to witness the country's stunning natural beauty firsthand.

Quick Facts

Population: 364,134
Capital City: Reykjavik
Language: Icelandic
Currency: Icelandic króna (ISK)
Time Zone: GMT (UTC+00:00)

Climate

Iceland has a subarctic climate, with cool temperatures throughout the year. Average temperatures range from -1°C (30°F) in winter to 11°C (52°F) in summer. The country is known for its dramatic weather patterns, including frequent rain, snow, and wind, as well as the occasional Northern Lights display in the winter months.

Top Attractions

Stanley Park

One of the largest urban parks in North America, Stanley Park is a lush oasis in the heart of Vancouver. Visitors can explore the park's forests, beaches, and scenic seawall, and check out attractions like the Vancouver Aquarium and the totem poles.

Capilano Suspension Bridge

This iconic suspension bridge spans 140 meters across the Capilano River Canyon and offers stunning views of the surrounding forest. Visitors can also check out the treetop adventure course, cliffwalk, and other attractions at the park.

Grouse Mountain

Grouse Mountain is a popular year-round destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can take a scenic gondola ride to the mountaintop and enjoy activities like skiing, snowboarding, hiking, ziplining, and wildlife encounters.

Granville Island

This vibrant neighbourhood is home to a bustling public market, artisan studios, and a variety of shops and restaurants. Visitors can also take a boat tour, watch street performers, or check out a show at one of the island's theatres.

Food & Drink

Icelandic Lamb

Iceland is known for its delicious lamb dishes. Try it at restaurants like Fiskmarkaðurinn, Grillmarkaðurinn, or Matur og Drykkur.

Icelandic Fish

Iceland's cold, clean waters make it an ideal place for fresh seafood. Check out restaurants like Fiskfélagið, Sjávarkjallarinn, or Fish Market.

Skyr

Skyr is a traditional Icelandic dairy product that's similar to yogurt. You can find it at grocery stores and cafes all over Iceland.

Hotels & Accommodation

Icelandair Hotel Reykjavik Marina

This stylish hotel is located in Reykjavik's old harbor and features colorful decor, a bar, and a restaurant serving Icelandic cuisine.

Frost and Fire Hotel

This hotel is located in the town of Hveragerði, known for its hot springs and geothermal activity. It features hot tubs and a restaurant with views of the nearby river.

ION Adventure Hotel

This eco-friendly hotel is located near Thingvellir National Park and features a spa, a restaurant serving Nordic cuisine, and a bar with views of the Northern Lights.

Getting Around

Bus

Iceland's bus system, called Strætó, provides transportation throughout the country, including to popular tourist destinations like the Blue Lagoon and the Golden Circle.

Car Rental

Renting a car is a popular way to explore Iceland's scenic landscapes. Car rental companies are available at Keflavik International Airport and throughout the country.
