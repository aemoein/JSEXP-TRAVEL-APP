Kyoto

Kyoto is a city located in the Kansai region of Japan. It is known for its rich history and culture, as well as its stunning temples and shrines. Visitors to Kyoto can explore the city's many historic landmarks, including the famous Kiyomizu-dera temple, as well as its beautiful gardens and traditional neighborhoods. Kyoto is also renowned for its cuisine, including specialties like tofu, matcha, and kaiseki cuisine.

Quick Facts

Population: 1.5 million
Region: Kansai
Time Zone: Japan Standard Time (UTC+9)
Language: Japanese
Currency: Japanese yen (JPY)

Top Attractions

Kinkaku-ji

Also known as the Golden Pavilion, Kinkaku-ji is a Zen Buddhist temple famous for its stunning architecture and beautiful garden. The pavilion is covered in gold leaf, creating a breathtaking sight reflected in the surrounding pond.

Fushimi Inari-taisha

This Shinto shrine is known for its thousands of torii gates that form a winding path through the forested hills behind the main buildings. Visitors can hike the trail to the summit for stunning views of Kyoto.

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

This serene bamboo forest is a popular destination for visitors to Kyoto. The towering bamboo stalks create a peaceful and calming atmosphere as visitors walk through the forest.

Kiyomizu-dera

Founded in 778, Kiyomizu-dera is one of the most celebrated temples in Japan. The main hall is built on a steep slope, providing visitors with panoramic views of the city. The temple is particularly beautiful during the cherry blossom and autumn foliage seasons.

Food & Drink

Sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish that is popular in Kyoto. Some of the best places to try sushi in Kyoto are Izuju, Kaiten-zushi Musashi, and Ganko Sushi.

Matcha

Matcha is a powdered green tea that is commonly used in Japanese tea ceremonies. Kyoto is known for its high-quality matcha, and some great places to try it are Tsujiri, Gion Tsujiri, and Ippodo Tea Co.

Yudofu

Yudofu is a simple tofu dish that is often served with a light dipping sauce. It's a popular dish in Kyoto, and some of the best places to try it are Yudofu Sagano, Tousuiro, and Nishiki Warai.

Getting Around

City Bus

The city bus is a convenient way to get around Kyoto, with many routes that stop at major tourist attractions. Bus fare is typically paid when exiting the bus.

Bicycle

Bicycles are a popular way to explore Kyoto, and there are many rental shops around the city. Some popular cycling routes include the Philosopher's Path and the Kamo River.
