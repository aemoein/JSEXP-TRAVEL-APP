Tokyo

Tokyo is a bustling metropolis that combines traditional Japanese culture with modern innovation. Known for its skyscrapers, neon lights, and bustling streets, Tokyo is an exciting and vibrant city that offers something for everyone. Visitors can experience the city's unique blend of old and new by exploring its historic temples and gardens, as well as its cutting-edge technology and entertainment. Tokyo is also a foodie's paradise, with a diverse range of restaurants serving up delicious Japanese cuisine. Whether you're interested in culture, shopping, or nightlife, Tokyo has plenty to offer.

Top Attractions

Senso-ji Temple

One of Tokyo's oldest and most significant temples, Senso-ji is a popular destination for both tourists and locals. Visitors can explore the temple grounds, enjoy street food in the nearby Nakamise shopping street, and even take a rickshaw tour of the area.

Tokyo Tower

This iconic tower offers stunning views of the city from its observation decks. Visitors can also check out the aquarium, wax museum, and various shops and restaurants located within the tower's complex.

Shibuya Crossing

One of the busiest intersections in the world, Shibuya Crossing is a must-see for anyone visiting Tokyo. Visitors can experience the rush of crossing the intersection with hundreds of other people, and also explore the nearby shops, restaurants, and nightlife.

Meiji Shrine

Dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken, this shrine is a peaceful oasis in the heart of Tokyo. Visitors can take a stroll through the tranquil forest surrounding the shrine, and participate in traditional Shinto rituals such as writing wishes on wooden plaques.

Food & Drink

Sushi

Sushi is a Japanese dish made of vinegared rice and various ingredients, including raw fish or seafood. Some of the best places to try sushi in Tokyo are Sukiyabashi Jiro, Sushi Saito, and Kyubey.

Ramen

Ramen is a Japanese noodle soup dish that typically contains Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a meat or fish-based broth. Some of the best places to try ramen in Tokyo are Ichiran, Afuri, and Tsuta.

Sake

Sake is a Japanese rice wine that's brewed using a fermentation process. Some of the best places to enjoy sake in Tokyo are Sasashigure, Kurand Sake Market, and Kuri.

Hotels & Accommodation

Park Hyatt Tokyo

This luxury hotel is located in the heart of Tokyo and offers spacious rooms and suites with panoramic city views. Amenities include a spa, fitness center, and several dining options.

Andaz Tokyo

This chic hotel is situated in the Toranomon Hills complex and offers stylish rooms and suites with modern amenities. Guests can also enjoy the on-site spa, rooftop bar, and restaurant.

Capsule Hotel

This unique hotel concept offers small, capsule-like rooms for a more affordable and minimalist stay. Some popular capsule hotels in Tokyo include 9h Ninehours, First Cabin, and The Millennials Shibuya.

Getting Around

Subway

The Tokyo Metro is a rapid transit system that provides quick and easy access to most of the city's attractions. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

Bike

Biking is a popular way to explore Tokyo's neighborhoods and parks. Several bike rental companies are available, including Tokyo Bike Rental, Cycle Tokyo, and Rent a Bike Today.
