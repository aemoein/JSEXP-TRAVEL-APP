New York

New York City is one of the most vibrant and exciting cities in the world. Located in the northeastern United States, it is home to some of the world's most famous landmarks, including the Statue of Liberty, the Empire State Building, and Central Park. With its diverse neighborhoods, world-renowned museums, and thriving arts and culture scene, there's something for everyone in the Big Apple. Visitors can enjoy delicious cuisine from around the world, catch a Broadway show, or simply explore the city's many attractions.

Quick Facts

Population: 8.3 million
State: New York
Time Zone: Eastern Time Zone (UTC-05:00)
Language: English
Currency: US dollar (USD)

Climate

New York City has a humid subtropical climate with four distinct seasons. Summers are hot and humid, with average temperatures in the mid to high 80s Fahrenheit (around 30 degrees Celsius). Winters are cold and snowy, with average temperatures in the 30s Fahrenheit (around 0 degrees Celsius). Spring and fall are mild and pleasant. Visitors should pack accordingly depending on the time of year they plan to visit.

Top Attractions

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a famous symbol of freedom and a must-visit attraction in New York. Visitors can take a ferry to Liberty Island and enjoy stunning views of the New York skyline from the crown of the statue.

Central Park

Located in the heart of Manhattan, Central Park is a massive urban park with a wide range of attractions, including lakes, gardens, museums, and sports facilities. Visitors can also take a horse-drawn carriage ride or rent a bike to explore the park.

Empire State Building

The Empire State Building is an iconic skyscraper that offers breathtaking views of the city from its observation deck. Visitors can also learn about the building's history and construction at the on-site museum.

Brooklyn Bridge

This historic suspension bridge connects Manhattan and Brooklyn and offers spectacular views of the city skyline. Visitors can walk or bike across the bridge and take in the sights and sounds of New York.

Times Square

This iconic commercial and entertainment hub is known for its bright lights, billboards, and bustling atmosphere. Visitors can take in a Broadway show, shop, or dine at one of the many restaurants in the area.

One World Trade Center

The tallest building in the Western Hemisphere, One World Trade Center is a powerful symbol of New York's resilience and strength. Visitors can take in sweeping views of the city from the observation deck on the 100th floor.

Metropolitan Museum of Art

One of the world's largest and most comprehensive art museums, the Met is home to an impressive collection of art from around the globe. Visitors can explore the galleries and exhibitions, attend lectures or performances, or relax in the museum's picturesque gardens.

American Museum of Natural History

This world-renowned museum is dedicated to the study of science and natural history. Visitors can explore the exhibits, including the famous dinosaur fossils and the Rose Center for Earth and Space.

Grand Central Terminal

This historic train station is not just a transportation hub, but also a destination in its own right. Visitors can admire the Beaux-Arts architecture, shop or dine in the market, or take a guided tour of the station.

High Line

This unique public park is built on an elevated railway line that runs through Manhattan's West Side. Visitors can walk along the park's paths, enjoy the views, and take in the public art installations.

Rockefeller Center

This iconic complex in the heart of Midtown Manhattan is known for its stunning Art Deco architecture, shopping, and dining options. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the center and the famous Rockefeller Plaza.

Broadway

The world-famous theater district is home to some of the most beloved musicals and plays of all time. Visitors can catch a show at one of the many theaters on Broadway or the surrounding streets.

Food & Drink

Pizza

New York is famous for its pizza, and some of the best places to try it are Di Fara Pizza, Lombardi's, and Patsy's Pizzeria.

Bagel

Another New York classic is the bagel, and some of the best spots to get one are Russ & Daughters, H&H Bagels, and Ess-a-Bagel.

Pastrami Sandwich

A classic New York deli sandwich made with thinly sliced pastrami on rye bread. Some popular delis that serve this sandwich include Katz's Delicatessen, Carnegie Deli, and 2nd Ave Deli.

Hot Dogs

Street vendors selling hot dogs are a common sight in New York City. Some popular places to try them include Gray's Papaya, Nathan's Famous, and Papaya King.

Cheesecake

New York-style cheesecake is a dense, creamy dessert that's typically made with cream cheese and a graham cracker crust. Some popular places to try it include Junior's, Eileen's Special Cheesecake, and Veniero's.

Cronut

A hybrid pastry that's part croissant, part doughnut. The original Cronut was created by Dominique Ansel Bakery and can still be found there, as well as at other bakeries around the city.

Halal Cart Food

Halal cart food is a popular street food in NYC and can be found on many street corners. Some of the most popular dishes include chicken over rice, lamb over rice, and falafel.

Cocktails

New York has a thriving cocktail scene, with plenty of speakeasies and rooftop bars to choose from. Some popular spots include The Dead Rabbit, PDT, and The Roof.

Hotels & Accommodation

The Plaza

This iconic hotel is located on Fifth Avenue and offers luxurious rooms and suites with classic decor. Amenities include a spa, fitness center, and several dining options.

The Langham, New York, Fifth Avenue

This luxurious hotel is located on Fifth Avenue in Midtown Manhattan and features elegant rooms and suites with modern amenities. Amenities include a spa, fitness center, and multiple dining options.

The St. Regis New York

This iconic hotel is located in Midtown Manhattan and offers luxurious rooms and suites with upscale amenities. Amenities include a spa, fitness center, and fine dining options.

The Peninsula New York

This elegant hotel is located in Midtown Manhattan and features luxurious rooms and suites with high-end amenities. Amenities include a spa, fitness center, and multiple dining options, including a rooftop bar with panoramic views of the city.

The Standard

This trendy hotel is situated in the Meatpacking District and offers stylish rooms and suites with modern amenities. Guests can also enjoy the on-site rooftop bar, restaurant, and fitness center.

Pod 51 Hotel

This budget-friendly hotel is located in Midtown and offers compact rooms with shared bathrooms. Amenities include a rooftop terrace and lounge, and a cafe.

Getting Around

Subway

The subway is New York's rapid transit system, providing quick and easy access to most of the city's attractions. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

Citi Bike

New York's bike share program, Citi Bike, has thousands of bikes available for rent at various stations throughout the city. Users can sign up for a membership or purchase a day pass.
