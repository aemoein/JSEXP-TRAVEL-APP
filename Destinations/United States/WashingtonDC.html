Washington D.C.

Washington DC is the capital city of the United States and is located in the mid-Atlantic region of the country. It is known for its rich history, iconic landmarks, and diverse culture. The city is home to famous monuments and museums such as the Washington Monument, Lincoln Memorial, and the Smithsonian Institution, as well as important government buildings like the White House and the US Capitol.

Top Attractions

National Mall

The National Mall is a large park in the heart of Washington, DC, home to many of the city's most famous monuments and memorials, including the Lincoln Memorial, Washington Monument, and the World War II Memorial.

Smithsonian Institution

The Smithsonian Institution is a group of museums and research centers, with 19 museums and galleries, and the National Zoological Park, that offer free admission to visitors. The museums feature a diverse range of exhibits including art, history, culture, and science.

United States Capitol

The United States Capitol is the home of the United States Congress and is one of the most iconic buildings in the city. Visitors can take a tour of the building and learn about the history of the United States government.

National Gallery of Art

The National Gallery of Art is a world-class art museum that features a vast collection of paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts from around the world. Visitors can enjoy works by artists such as Leonardo da Vinci, Rembrandt, and Vincent van Gogh.

Washington Monument

The Washington Monument is an obelisk that stands at the center of the National Mall and honors the first President of the United States, George Washington. Visitors can take an elevator to the top for stunning views of the city.

National Air and Space Museum

The National Air and Space Museum is one of the most popular museums in the city, featuring a vast collection of aircraft, spacecraft, and other artifacts related to aviation and space travel. Visitors can see the Wright Brothers' airplane and the Apollo 11 command module, among other exhibits.

National Museum of African American History and Culture

The National Museum of African American History and Culture is the newest museum on the National Mall, dedicated to telling the story of African American history, culture, and achievements. The museum features a range of exhibits, including interactive displays and multimedia presentations.

National Museum of American History

The National Museum of American History is another popular museum on the National Mall, showcasing the history and culture of the United States. Visitors can see the original Star-Spangled Banner, the First Ladies' Inaugural Gowns, and many other artifacts that tell the story of America.

Hotels & Accommodation

This iconic hotel has hosted numerous celebrities and offers luxurious accommodations, a spa, and multiple dining options.

This trendy hotel is located in the heart of downtown Los Angeles and offers a rooftop pool, multiple dining options, and a 24-hour fitness center.

Getting Around

Metro

The Metro is Los Angeles' rapid transit system, providing access to popular destinations throughout the city. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

Bike Share

Los Angeles also has a bike share program, with hundreds of bikes available for rent at various stations throughout the city. Users can sign up for a membership or purchase a day pass.

Rental Car

If you prefer to drive, rental cars are available at the airport and throughout the city. Just be prepared for traffic!
