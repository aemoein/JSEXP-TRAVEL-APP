New Orleans

New Orleans is a city located in the southern part of Louisiana in the United States. Known for its vibrant music scene, unique cuisine, and rich cultural heritage, New Orleans is a popular destination for tourists and visitors from around the world. The city is home to iconic attractions such as the French Quarter, Bourbon Street, and the Jazz Fest, as well as historic landmarks like the St. Louis Cathedral and the National World War II Museum.

Top Attractions

French Quarter

The heart of New Orleans, the French Quarter is known for its colorful buildings, lively music scene, and delicious cuisine. Be sure to visit Jackson Square, St. Louis Cathedral, and the historic Cafe Du Monde for some beignets and coffee.

National WWII Museum

This world-class museum is dedicated to telling the story of the American experience in World War II. Exhibits include artifacts, personal accounts, and interactive displays that bring the history to life.

Garden District

Take a stroll through this picturesque neighborhood and admire the stunning homes and gardens. Be sure to visit Lafayette Cemetery No. 1, which has been featured in many movies and TV shows.

New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival

This annual festival, also known as Jazz Fest, is a celebration of the city's music, culture, and food. Held every spring, it features performances by some of the biggest names in jazz, blues, and other genres, as well as delicious local cuisine.

Mardi Gras

This world-famous festival, held annually in February or March, is a celebration of music, parades, and revelry. Be sure to catch some beads and experience the unique traditions and culture of New Orleans.

Audubon Zoo

This family-friendly attraction features a variety of animals from around the world, including elephants, lions, and giraffes. The zoo also offers a train ride, a carousel, and a water park for visitors to enjoy.

Steamboat Natchez

Take a scenic cruise down the Mississippi River on this authentic steamboat, which offers live jazz music and delicious Creole cuisine. Visitors can also learn about the history and culture of New Orleans while enjoying the beautiful views.

City Park

This 1,300-acre park is one of the largest urban parks in the country, offering a variety of attractions like the New Orleans Museum of Art, a botanical garden, a golf course, and even a sculpture garden featuring works by local artists.

Food & Drink

Burgers

Los Angeles has plenty of great burger joints to choose from, including In-N-Out Burger, Umami Burger, and Father's Office.

Tacos

With its close proximity to Mexico, Los Angeles has some of the best tacos around. Check out Guisados, Leo's Tacos Truck, and Tacos 1986.

Sushi

For some delicious sushi, head to Sugarfish, Sushi Gen, or KazuNori.

Hotels & Accommodation

The Beverly Hills Hotel

This iconic hotel has hosted numerous celebrities and offers luxurious accommodations, a spa, and multiple dining options.

The Standard, Downtown LA

This trendy hotel is located in the heart of downtown Los Angeles and offers a rooftop pool, multiple dining options, and a 24-hour fitness center.

Ace Hotel Downtown LA

This boutique hotel features unique decor and a rooftop bar with stunning views of the city skyline.

Getting Around

Metro

The Metro is Los Angeles' rapid transit system, providing access to popular destinations throughout the city. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

Bike Share

Los Angeles also has a bike share program, with hundreds of bikes available for rent at various stations throughout the city. Users can sign up for a membership or purchase a day pass.

Rental Car

If you prefer to drive, rental cars are available at the airport and throughout the city. Just be prepared for traffic!
