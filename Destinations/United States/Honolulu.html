Honolulu is the capital city of Hawaii, a state located in the Pacific Ocean. Known for its stunning beaches, clear waters, and rich cultural heritage, Honolulu is a popular destination for tourists and visitors from around the world. The city is home to iconic attractions such as Waikiki Beach, Diamond Head, and the Pearl Harbor National Memorial, as well as historic landmarks like Iolani Palace and the Bishop Museum.

Top Attractions

Waikiki Beach

This famous beach is a must-visit for anyone coming to Honolulu, with its crystal-clear waters, soft sand, and perfect surfing conditions. Visitors can also take a stroll along the boardwalk and enjoy the stunning views of Diamond Head.

Pearl Harbor National Memorial

This historic site is a tribute to the sailors and Marines who lost their lives during the attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941. Visitors can tour the USS Arizona Memorial and the Pearl Harbor Visitor Center to learn more about this pivotal moment in American history.

Diamond Head State Monument

Hike to the top of Diamond Head and take in the stunning views of Honolulu and the surrounding ocean. This volcanic crater is one of the most iconic landmarks on the island and a must-see for outdoor enthusiasts.

Iolani Palace

This historic palace was the residence of Hawaii's last monarchs, King Kalakaua and Queen Liliuokalani. Today, visitors can take a guided tour of the palace and learn about Hawaii's rich history and culture.

Honolulu Zoo

Located in Waikiki, the Honolulu Zoo is home to a variety of exotic animals from around the world, including elephants, tigers, and chimpanzees. Visitors can also attend animal encounters, watch animal feedings, and take part in educational programs.

Bishop Museum

This world-renowned museum features exhibits on Hawaiian history, culture, and natural sciences. Visitors can see artifacts like ancient Hawaiian weapons and tools, as well as learn about the plants and animals that call Hawaii home.

Hanauma Bay

Snorkel in the crystal-clear waters of Hanauma Bay and explore the vibrant marine life that calls this protected nature preserve home. Visitors can also relax on the beach and take in the stunning views of the surrounding cliffs and ocean.

USS Bowfin Submarine Museum

Step aboard the USS Bowfin, a World War II submarine, and learn about the daily life of the crew during wartime. The museum also features exhibits on other historic submarines and the history of naval warfare.

Food & Drink

Burgers

Los Angeles has plenty of great burger joints to choose from, including In-N-Out Burger, Umami Burger, and Father's Office.

Tacos

With its close proximity to Mexico, Los Angeles has some of the best tacos around. Check out Guisados, Leo's Tacos Truck, and Tacos 1986.

Sushi

For some delicious sushi, head to Sugarfish, Sushi Gen, or KazuNori.

Hotels & Accommodation

The Beverly Hills Hotel

This iconic hotel has hosted numerous celebrities and offers luxurious accommodations, a spa, and multiple dining options.

The Standard, Downtown LA

This trendy hotel is located in the heart of downtown Los Angeles and offers a rooftop pool, multiple dining options, and a 24-hour fitness center.

Ace Hotel Downtown LA

This boutique hotel features unique decor and a rooftop bar with stunning views of the city skyline.

Getting Around

Metro

The Metro is Los Angeles' rapid transit system, providing access to popular destinations throughout the city. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

Bike Share

Los Angeles also has a bike share program, with hundreds of bikes available for rent at various stations throughout the city. Users can sign up for a membership or purchase a day pass.

Rental Car

If you prefer to drive, rental cars are available at the airport and throughout the city. Just be prepared for traffic!
