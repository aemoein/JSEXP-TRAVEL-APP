Montreal

Montreal is a vibrant city located in the province of Quebec, Canada. Known for its European charm and rich history, Montreal is a city that offers a unique blend of cultures, languages, and experiences. Visitors can explore the city's cobblestone streets, quaint cafes, and historic architecture, or take in the many festivals and events that Montreal is famous for. Montreal is also a foodie's paradise, with an incredible culinary scene that draws inspiration from around the world. Whether you're looking for culture, history, or cuisine, Montreal is a destination that has something for everyone.

Quick Facts

Population: 1.7 million
Province: Quebec
Time Zone: Eastern Time Zone (UTC-05:00)
Language: French, English
Currency: Canadian dollar (CAD)

Climate

Montreal has a humid continental climate with warm summers and cold winters. Average temperatures range from -9°C (15°F) in winter to 26°C (79°F) in summer, with occasional heat waves and heavy snowfall in the winter months. The best time to visit Montreal is in the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and the city is alive with festivals and events.

Top Attractions

Old Montreal

Explore the charming cobblestone streets and historic buildings of this picturesque neighborhood, which dates back to the 17th century. Be sure to check out the stunning Notre-Dame Basilica and the bustling Bonsecours Market.

Mont Royal Park

This expansive park offers stunning views of the city from its namesake mountain. Visitors can hike or bike the many trails, enjoy a picnic or barbecue, or simply relax and take in the scenery.

Montreal Museum of Fine Arts

This world-renowned museum boasts an impressive collection of more than 43,000 works of art, ranging from ancient artifacts to contemporary pieces. Visitors can also enjoy concerts, films, and other cultural events.

Botanical Garden

Explore the lush gardens and greenhouses of this peaceful oasis, which features over 22,000 plant species from around the world. Don't miss the stunning Chinese Garden, which is one of the largest of its kind outside of China.

Food & Drink

Poutine

Poutine is a Canadian dish made with french fries, gravy, and cheese curds. Some of the best places to try poutine in Montreal are La Banquise, Chez Claudette, and Poutineville.

Bagels

Montreal-style bagels are smaller and sweeter than their New York counterparts, and are boiled in honey water before being baked in a wood-fired oven. Some of the best places to try bagels in Montreal are St-Viateur Bagel, Fairmount Bagel, and Dizz's Bagel & Deli.

Maple Syrup

Canada is known for its maple syrup, and Montreal is no exception. Some of the best places to enjoy maple syrup in Montreal are Sucrerie de la Montagne, Cabane à Sucre Au Pied de Cochon, and La Petite Cabane à Sucre de Québec.

Hotels & Accommodation

Hotel William Gray

This stylish hotel is located in Old Montreal and offers luxurious rooms and suites with modern amenities. Amenities include a rooftop terrace, fitness center, and several dining options.

Le Mount Stephen

This historic hotel is situated in the Golden Square Mile and offers elegant rooms and suites with classic decor. Guests can also enjoy the on-site spa, restaurant, and bar.

Auberge du Vieux-Port

This charming hotel is located in Old Montreal and offers cozy rooms and suites with rustic decor. Amenities include a rooftop terrace, restaurant, and bar.

Getting Around

Metro

The metro is Montreal's rapid transit system, providing quick and easy access to most of the city's attractions. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

BIXI

BIXI is Montreal's bike share program, with thousands of bikes available for rent at various stations throughout the city. Users can sign up for a membership or purchase a day pass.
