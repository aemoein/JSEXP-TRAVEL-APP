Vancouver is a vibrant and diverse city located on the west coast of Canada. Known for its stunning natural beauty, Vancouver is surrounded by mountains, forests, and beaches, offering endless opportunities for outdoor activities. The city is also home to a thriving arts and culture scene, with world-class museums, galleries, and theaters. Visitors can explore the city's diverse neighborhoods, each with its own unique character and charm, or enjoy the delicious cuisine that Vancouver is famous for. Whether you're looking for adventure, relaxation, or a little bit of both, Vancouver has something to offer everyone.

Quick Facts

Population: 647,540
Province: British Columbia
Time Zone: Pacific Time Zone (UTC-08:00)
Language: English, French
Currency: Canadian dollar (CAD)

Climate

Vancouver has a mild, temperate climate with moderate rainfall throughout the year. Average temperatures range from 2°C (36°F) in winter to 19°C (66°F) in summer, with plenty of sunshine and opportunities for outdoor activities year-round. Remember to pack layers for changing weather conditions.

Top Attractions

Stanley Park

One of the largest urban parks in North America, Stanley Park is a lush oasis in the heart of Vancouver. Visitors can explore the park's forests, beaches, and scenic seawall, and check out attractions like the Vancouver Aquarium and the totem poles.

Capilano Suspension Bridge

This iconic suspension bridge spans 140 meters across the Capilano River Canyon and offers stunning views of the surrounding forest. Visitors can also check out the treetop adventure course, cliffwalk, and other attractions at the park.

Grouse Mountain

Grouse Mountain is a popular year-round destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can take a scenic gondola ride to the mountaintop and enjoy activities like skiing, snowboarding, hiking, ziplining, and wildlife encounters.

Granville Island

This vibrant neighbourhood is home to a bustling public market, artisan studios, and a variety of shops and restaurants. Visitors can also take a boat tour, watch street performers, or check out a show at one of the island's theatres.

Food & Drink

Sushi

Vancouver is known for its fresh seafood and sushi is a popular choice for locals and visitors alike. Some top spots to try include Miku, Tojo's, and Sushi Bar Maumi.

Craft Beer

Vancouver is also home to a thriving craft beer scene, with a variety of local breweries and taprooms. Some popular options include Brassneck Brewery, Main Street Brewing, and Strange Fellows Brewing.

Poutine

This quintessential Canadian dish consists of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy. Some top spots to try include Fritz European Fry House, La Belle Patate, and Smoke's Poutinerie.

Cairo is a bustling metropolis and the capital city of Egypt. It is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning architecture. Home to some of the world's most famous landmarks, such as the Great Pyramids and the Sphinx, Cairo is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in ancient Egyptian civilization. The city also offers a wealth of modern attractions, including shopping malls, restaurants, and nightlife. Cairo is a city of contrasts, where you can experience the ancient and the modern side by side.

Quick Facts

Population: 20,901,000
Country: Egypt
Time Zone: Eastern European Time (UTC+02:00)
Language: Arabic
Currency: Egyptian pound (EGP)

Climate

Cairo has a hot desert climate with very little rainfall throughout the year. Summers are extremely hot and dry, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F). Winters are mild and sunny, with temperatures ranging from 10°C (50°F) to 20°C (68°F). It is recommended to visit Cairo in the cooler months from November to March to avoid the extreme heat.

Getting Around

SkyTrain

The SkyTrain is Vancouver's rapid transit system, providing quick and convenient access to downtown, the airport, and other key destinations. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

Bike Share

Vancouver also has a bike share program, with hundreds of bikes available for rent at various stations throughout the city. Users can sign up for a membership or purchase a day pass.

Ferry

For those looking to explore the surrounding areas, ferries are available from Vancouver to destinations like Victoria, the Gulf Islands, and the Sunshine Coast. Reservations are recommended, especially during peak season.
