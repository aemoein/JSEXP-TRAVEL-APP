Namibia

Namibia is a country located in southern Africa, known for its stunning natural beauty and diverse wildlife. From the towering sand dunes of Sossusvlei to the rugged terrain of Etosha National Park, Namibia offers a range of landscapes and outdoor activities for visitors to explore. The country is also home to many indigenous cultures, with a rich history and traditions that can be seen in local art, music, and cuisine. Whether you're looking to embark on a safari adventure, relax on a sandy beach, or learn about local cultures, Namibia has something for everyone.

Quick Facts

Population: 2.54 million
Capital: Windhoek
Languages: English, Afrikaans, German, Oshiwambo, and other indigenous languages
Currency: Namibian dollar (NAD)
Time Zone: Central Africa Time (CAT; UTC+2)

Attractions

Etosha National Park

One of the largest game reserves in Africa, Etosha National Park is home to a wide variety of wildlife including lions, elephants, and rhinos.

Sossusvlei

Famous for its towering red sand dunes, Sossusvlei is a must-see destination for nature lovers and photographers.

Fish River Canyon

The second largest canyon in the world, Fish River Canyon offers breathtaking views and hiking trails for adventurous visitors.

Hotels & Accommodation

The Olive Exclusive

This boutique hotel in Windhoek offers luxurious suites, a gourmet restaurant, and a rooftop pool with stunning city views.

Mowani Mountain Camp

Nestled among the boulders of Damaraland, Mowani Mountain Camp offers unique tented accommodations and panoramic views of the desert landscape.

Sossusvlei Lodge

Located just outside the entrance to Sossusvlei, this lodge offers comfortable accommodations, a swimming pool, and guided tours of the area.

Getting Around

Car Rental

Renting a car is the best way to explore Namibia's vast and rugged landscapes. Major car rental companies operate at the airport and in major cities.

Bus

Buses are available for travel between major cities and towns, but they can be slow and uncomfortable. Minibus taxis are a cheaper alternative, but they can be crowded and unsafe.

Airplane

Domestic flights are available between major cities and towns, but they can be expensive. Charter flights are also an option for remote areas.
