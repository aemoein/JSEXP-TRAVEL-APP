Paris

Paris, the capital city of France, is one of the world's most popular tourist destinations. Known as the City of Light, Paris is famous for its stunning architecture, rich history, and art museums. Visitors can explore world-famous landmarks such as the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre Museum, and Notre-Dame Cathedral. Paris is also renowned for its cuisine, with a range of traditional French dishes and fine dining experiences available. Whether you're interested in art, history, culture, or food, Paris has something to offer everyone.

Quick Facts

Population: 2.1 million
Country: France
Time Zone: Central European Time (UTC+01:00)
Language: French
Currency: Euro (EUR)

Climate

Paris has a mild, temperate climate with warm summers and cool winters. The best time to visit is during the spring and fall, when the weather is pleasant and there are fewer crowds. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and damp. Be sure to check the weather forecast before packing.

Top Attractions

Eiffel Tower

The iconic Eiffel Tower is a must-see for visitors to Paris. Visitors can climb to the top for stunning views of the city, or simply admire the tower from below.

Louvre Museum

The Louvre Museum is home to thousands of works of art, including the famous Mona Lisa. Visitors can explore the museum's galleries and exhibitions, or take a guided tour.

Notre-Dame Cathedral

Despite suffering damage in a 2019 fire, Notre-Dame Cathedral remains a popular attraction in Paris. Visitors can admire the Gothic architecture and learn about the cathedral's history.

Montmartre and Sacré-Cœur Basilica

Montmartre is a charming neighborhood in Paris known for its artistic history and hilltop views. The Sacré-Cœur Basilica is a famous landmark located at the top of the hill, offering panoramic views of the city.

Palace of Versailles

The Palace of Versailles is a grandiose royal residence located just outside of Paris. Visitors can explore the opulent rooms and gardens of the palace, which was the center of political power in France during the 17th and 18th centuries.

Musée d'Orsay

The Musée d'Orsay is a renowned art museum located in a former train station in Paris. Its collection includes an impressive range of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist works, as well as decorative arts and photography.

Centre Pompidou

The Centre Pompidou is a modern art museum and cultural center in the heart of Paris. Its distinctive architecture and innovative exhibitions make it a must-visit destination for art enthusiasts.

Sainte-Chapelle

Sainte-Chapelle is a stunning Gothic chapel located on the Île de la Cité in the heart of Paris. Its towering stained glass windows and intricate architecture make it a breathtaking sight to behold.

Champs-Élysées

This famous avenue is lined with shops, cafes, and theaters, and is a popular destination for shopping and dining. Visitors can also check out the Arc de Triomphe at the end of the avenue.

Musée de l'Orangerie

The Musée de l'Orangerie is an art museum located in the Tuileries Garden. It features impressionist and post-impressionist paintings, including works by Monet, Renoir, and Picasso.

Les Invalides

Les Invalides is a complex of buildings in the 7th arrondissement of Paris. It houses several museums and monuments, including the tomb of Napoleon Bonaparte.

Musée Rodin

The Musée Rodin is a museum dedicated to the works of the French sculptor Auguste Rodin. It features many of his sculptures, including "The Thinker" and "The Kiss."

Food & Drink

Croissants

Croissants are a staple breakfast pastry in Paris. Some of the best places to try croissants in Paris are Pierre Hermé, Du Pain et Des Idées, and Blé Sucré.

Coq au Vin

Coq au Vin is a chicken dish cooked in red wine, bacon, and mushrooms. Some of the best places to try Coq au Vin in Paris are Chez L'Ami Jean, Le Comptoir du Relais, and Bistrot Paul Bert.

Steak Frites

Steak Frites is a dish of steak served with French fries. Some of the best places to try Steak Frites in Paris are Le Relais de l'Entrecôte, Le Severo, and Le Bistrot Paul Bert.

French Onion Soup

French Onion Soup is a hearty soup made with onions, beef broth, and cheese. Some of the best places to try French Onion Soup in Paris are Au Pied de Cochon, Les Philosophes, and Le Soufflé.

Macarons

Macarons are colorful and delicate French cookies with a sweet filling. Some of the best places to try macarons in Paris are Ladurée, Pierre Hermé, and Fauchon.

Crème Brûlée

Crème Brûlée is a creamy custard dessert with a caramelized sugar top. Some of the best places to try Crème Brûlée in Paris are Le Comptoir du Relais, L'Ami Jean, and Les Ombres.

Baguette

Baguette is a French bread that's perfect for making sandwiches. Paris has plenty of great bakeries to choose from, such as Maison Kayser, Poilâne, and Eric Kayser.

Café au Lait

Café au Lait is a popular French breakfast drink made with coffee and hot milk. It is typically served in a large bowl or mug. Some great places to try Café au Lait in Paris are Café de Flore, Les Deux Magots, and Café Bonaparte.

Parisian Hot Chocolate

Hot Chocolate, or chocolat chaud, is a rich and decadent drink made with melted chocolate and hot milk. Some of the best places to try Hot Chocolate in Paris are Angelina, Jacques Genin, and Un Dimanche à Paris.

Chocolat Chaud à l'Ancienne

Chocolat Chaud à l'Ancienne is a traditional French hot chocolate made with dark chocolate and heavy cream. It is typically thicker and richer than regular hot chocolate. Some great places to try Chocolat Chaud à l'Ancienne in Paris are Angelina, Un Dimanche à Paris, and Jean-Paul Hévin.

Hotels & Accommodation

Four Seasons Hotel George V

This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of Paris and offers elegant rooms and suites with modern amenities. Amenities include a spa, fitness center, and several dining options.

Ritz Paris

The Ritz Paris is a legendary hotel located in the heart of the city. It features opulent rooms and suites, several restaurants and bars, and a world-class spa.

Hôtel de Crillon, A Rosewood Hotel

Hôtel de Crillon, A Rosewood Hotel is a historic hotel that has been beautifully restored and modernized. It offers luxurious rooms and suites, several dining options, and a spa.

Mandarin Oriental, Paris

Mandarin Oriental, Paris is a five-star hotel located in the fashionable 1st arrondissement. It offers stylish rooms and suites, an excellent restaurant, and a world-class spa.

Hotel Lutetia

This historic hotel is situated in the Saint-Germain-des-Prés neighborhood and offers stylish rooms and suites with Art Deco decor. Guests can also enjoy the on-site spa, fitness center, and restaurant.

Generator Hostel

This trendy hostel is located in the 10th arrondissement and offers dormitory and private rooms with shared bathrooms. Amenities include a bar, terrace, and game room.

Getting Around

Metro

The metro is Paris's rapid transit system, providing quick and easy access to most of the city's attractions. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

Vélib'

Vélib' is Paris's bike share program, with thousands of bikes available for rent at various stations throughout the city. Users can sign up for a membership or purchase a day pass.
