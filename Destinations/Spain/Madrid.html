Madrid

Madrid is the capital of Spain and a vibrant city known for its rich history, art, and culture. It is also known for its lively nightlife and world-class gastronomy. Madrid is home to many famous museums, including the Prado Museum and the Reina Sofia Museum, as well as beautiful parks and gardens such as Retiro Park. Visitors can explore the city's many charming neighborhoods, each with its own unique character and attractions, or enjoy the city's famous cuisine, including tapas and paella.

Quick Facts

Population: 3.3 million
Province: Madrid
Time Zone: Central European Time (UTC+01:00)
Language: Spanish
Currency: Euro (EUR)

Climate

Madrid has a Mediterranean climate with hot summers and cool winters. The average temperature in the summer is around 30°C (86°F), while in the winter it can drop to around 8°C (46°F). Rainfall is mostly concentrated in the autumn and spring, with occasional snowfall in the winter.

Top Attractions

Retiro Park

A beautiful park in the heart of Madrid, Retiro Park is a great place to relax and unwind. Visitors can take a boat ride on the park's lake, stroll through its gardens, and admire its impressive sculptures and monuments.

Prado Museum

One of the world's most famous art museums, the Prado Museum houses a vast collection of European art from the 12th to the 20th centuries. Visitors can see works by famous artists like Francisco Goya, Diego Velázquez, and El Greco.

Plaza Mayor

This beautiful square is one of Madrid's most popular attractions. Visitors can enjoy the picturesque architecture, visit the restaurants and cafes that surround the square, and check out the street performers and artists.

Royal Palace of Madrid

The official residence of the Spanish royal family, the Royal Palace of Madrid is a magnificent building with impressive interiors and beautiful gardens. Visitors can take a tour of the palace to learn about its history and see its many treasures.

Food & Drink

Cocido Madrileño

Cocido Madrileño is a traditional Madrid stew made with various meats, vegetables, and chickpeas. Some great places to try Cocido Madrileño in Madrid are La Bola Taberna, Malacatín, and Casa Carola.

Churros con Chocolate

Churros con Chocolate is a classic Spanish breakfast or snack consisting of long, thin, fried dough pastries dipped in thick hot chocolate. Some of the best places to try Churros con Chocolate in Madrid are Chocolatería San Ginés, Valor Chocolaterías, and Los Artesanos 1902.

Sangria

Sangria is a fruity and refreshing alcoholic beverage that's popular throughout Spain. Some great places to enjoy Sangria in Madrid are Cervecería Alemana, Mercado San Miguel, and El Tigre.

Hotels & Accommodation

Gran Meliá Palacio de los Duques

This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of Madrid and offers elegant rooms and suites with modern amenities. Amenities include a rooftop terrace, spa, and several dining options.

Hotel Orfila

This boutique hotel is situated in the Chamberí district and offers stylish rooms and suites with classic decor. Guests can also enjoy the on-site garden, restaurant, and library.

Hostal Persal

This budget-friendly hotel is located in the Puerta del Sol neighborhood and offers clean and comfortable rooms with private bathrooms. Amenities include a 24-hour front desk and free Wi-Fi.

Getting Around

Metro

The metro is Madrid's rapid transit system, providing quick and easy access to most of the city's attractions. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the mobile app.

BiciMAD

BiciMAD is Madrid's bike share program, with hundreds of bikes available for rent at various stations throughout the city. Users can sign up for a membership or purchase a day pass.
