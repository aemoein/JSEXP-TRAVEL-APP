Amsterdam

Amsterdam is the capital city of the Netherlands and a hub for art, culture, and commerce. The city's charming canals, picturesque bridges, and historic architecture draw millions of visitors each year. Amsterdam is known for its world-class museums, including the Van Gogh Museum, the Rijksmuseum, and the Anne Frank House. Visitors can also explore the city's vibrant neighborhoods, each with its own unique character and attractions. With its bike-friendly streets, bustling street markets, and diverse cuisine, Amsterdam has something to offer for everyone.

Top Attractions

I amsterdam sign

This iconic sign is a must-see attraction in Amsterdam. Visitors can take a photo in front of the giant red and white letters, located in front of the Rijksmuseum.

Red Light District

One of Amsterdam's most famous areas, the Red Light District is known for its legalized prostitution and coffee shops. Visitors can explore the narrow streets and alleys, and learn about the district's history and culture.

Van Gogh Museum

Dedicated to the life and work of Vincent Van Gogh, this museum houses the largest collection of the artist's paintings and drawings in the world. Visitors can learn about Van Gogh's life and art, and see some of his most famous works up close.

Dam Square

This bustling square is the heart of Amsterdam, and home to several important landmarks including the Royal Palace, the Nieuwe Kerk, and the National Monument. Visitors can take a walking tour of the area, or relax in one of the many cafes and restaurants.

Food & Drink

Stroopwafel

A stroopwafel is a Dutch treat made from two thin waffles with a caramel filling. Some top spots to try include Lanskroon, Van Wonderen Stroopwafels, and Original Stroopwafels stand at Albert Cuyp Market.

Poffertjes

Poffertjes are small, fluffy pancakes typically served with butter and powdered sugar. Some popular places to try them include Pancakes Amsterdam, The Pancake Bakery, and De Vier Pilaren.

Jenever

Jenever is a traditional Dutch gin-like liquor. Some great places to try it include Wynand Fockink, Proeflokaal A. van Wees, and De Drie Fleschjes.

Hotels & Accommodation

Amsterdam Marriott Hotel

This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of Amsterdam and offers spacious rooms and suites, a fitness center, and multiple dining options.

Hotel Pulitzer Amsterdam

This boutique hotel is situated in the historic Canal District and offers unique rooms and suites, a garden terrace, and a restaurant and bar.

Lloyd Hotel

This design hotel is located in a historic building and offers rooms and suites with eclectic decor, a restaurant, and a bar.

Getting Around

Bike

Biking is a popular way to get around Amsterdam, with bike rental shops located throughout the city. Some top places to rent a bike include MacBike, Starbikes Rental, and Yellow Bike.

Tram

Amsterdam's tram system is an efficient way to get around the city. Tickets can be purchased at stations or through the GVB app.

Boat

Exploring Amsterdam's canals by boat is a unique and scenic way to see the amazing attractions that Amsterdam has to offer
