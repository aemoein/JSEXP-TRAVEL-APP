Porto

Porto is a historic and vibrant city located in northern Portugal. Known for its beautiful architecture, charming streets, and rich culture, Porto is a must-visit destination for travelers. The city is also home to the Douro River, which winds through the city and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and vineyards. Visitors can explore the city's many museums, galleries, and landmarks, or indulge in the delicious food and wine that Porto is famous for. Whether you're looking for a relaxing getaway or an exciting adventure, Porto has something to offer everyone.

Quick Facts

Population: 237,559
Region: Norte Region
Time Zone: Western European Time (UTC+00:00)
Language: Portuguese
Currency: Euro (EUR)

Climate

Porto has a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. Average temperatures range from 6°C (43°F) in winter to 24°C (75°F) in summer, making it a great destination year-round. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain and pack accordingly.

Top Attractions

Porto Cathedral

One of the most iconic landmarks in Porto, the Porto Cathedral is a Romanesque-style church with a beautiful rose window and intricate interior decorations. Visitors can climb to the top of the cathedral for panoramic views of the city.

São Bento Railway Station

This historic railway station is famous for its stunning azulejo tile murals that depict scenes from Portuguese history. Visitors can admire the art and architecture of the station, as well as catch a train to other parts of Portugal.

Palácio da Bolsa

This ornate 19th-century building was once the stock exchange of Porto and is now a popular tourist attraction. Visitors can tour the grand halls and rooms, which feature intricate woodwork, frescoes, and chandeliers.

Livraria Lello

This beautiful bookstore is famous for its stunning neo-gothic architecture and its connection to author J.K. Rowling, who lived in Porto for a time. Visitors can browse the shelves of this historic bookstore and admire its ornate details.

Food & Drink

Francesinha

This iconic sandwich from Porto is made with layers of meat, cheese, and a spicy tomato sauce. Try it at Cafe Santiago, Cafe Majestic, or Bufete Fase.

Port Wine

Porto is famous for its port wine, which is made in the Douro Valley. Visit the port wine cellars in Vila Nova de Gaia for tastings and tours.

Seafood

With its location on the Atlantic coast, Porto has plenty of fresh seafood to enjoy. Check out O Gaveto, Restaurante O Escondidinho, or Cervejaria Gazela.

Hotels & Accommodation

Pestana Vintage Porto Hotel & World Heritage Site

This historic hotel is located in the Ribeira district, overlooking the Douro River. It features a restaurant, bar, and rooftop terrace.

InterContinental Porto - Palacio das Cardosas

This luxury hotel is housed in a renovated palace in the city center, and features a spa, fitness center, and gourmet restaurant.

PortoBay Flores

This elegant hotel is located in the historic district, and features a rooftop bar, indoor pool, and spa.

Getting Around

Metro

The Porto Metro is a convenient way to get around the city, with several lines and stations throughout the city center.

Tram

The historic trams in Porto are a fun way to explore the city's hills and narrow streets. Take tram 1 from the Ribeira to Foz do Douro for scenic views of the coast.

Bike

Porto has a bike share program called "Bicicletas Publicas," with stations located throughout the city. Users can purchase a day pass or sign up for a monthly membership
