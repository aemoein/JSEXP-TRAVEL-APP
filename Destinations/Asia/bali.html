Bali

Bali is a beautiful island located in Indonesia, known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and stunning landscapes. It is a popular destination for travelers seeking a tropical paradise with a unique culture and vibrant nightlife. Visitors to Bali can explore ancient temples, hike through lush jungles, surf world-renowned waves, or simply relax on the beach. Bali's warm climate and friendly locals make it an ideal destination for any traveler.

Quick Facts

Population: 4.3 million
Language: Indonesian, Balinese
Currency: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)
Time Zone: UTC+08:00
International Dialing Code: +62

Climate

Bali has a tropical climate with warm temperatures year-round. The rainy season lasts from October to March, with the most rain falling in December and January. The dry season is from April to September, with lower humidity and less rainfall. The best time to visit Bali is during the dry season, when the weather is sunny and the beaches are perfect for swimming and water activities.

Top Attractions

Ubud Monkey Forest

A nature reserve and temple complex in Ubud that's home to hundreds of macaques. Visitors can stroll through lush greenery and marvel at the intricate stone carvings and architecture of the temples.

Tegalalang Rice Terraces

A UNESCO World Heritage Site located north of Ubud, known for its stunning terraced rice paddies that offer sweeping views of the lush green landscape. Visitors can take a leisurely hike or bike ride through the area and soak up the natural beauty.

Tanah Lot Temple

A Hindu temple situated on a rocky outcrop in the sea, offering panoramic views of the coastline and spectacular sunsets. Visitors can explore the temple grounds and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere.

Uluwatu Temple

A Balinese sea temple located atop a cliff, offering breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean. Visitors can watch traditional dance performances and explore the surrounding area.

Food & Drink

Nasi Goreng

Nasi Goreng is a traditional Indonesian fried rice dish that is a staple in Bali. Some of the best places to try Nasi Goreng in Bali are Warung Nasi Ayam Bu Oki, Warung Taman Bambu, and Warung Mak Beng.

Satay

Satay is a popular Indonesian street food that consists of grilled meat on skewers. Bali has many great places to try satay, such as Warung Babi Guling Pak Malen, Warung Rindu, and Warung Eny.

Bintang Beer

Bintang Beer is Bali's most popular local beer, and it's the perfect drink to enjoy on a hot day. You can find Bintang Beer at most bars and restaurants throughout the island.

Hotels & Accommodation

The Mulia

This luxury beachfront resort in Nusa Dua offers spacious suites and villas with private pools and ocean views. Amenities include a spa, fitness center, and several dining options.

The Ubud Village Resort & Spa

This tranquil resort in Ubud offers beautiful villas surrounded by lush gardens and rice paddies. Guests can also enjoy the on-site spa, yoga studio, and restaurant.

Kuta Central Park Hotel

This modern hotel in Kuta offers comfortable rooms and suites with a pool, restaurant, and fitness center. It's also located near many restaurants and shopping areas.

Getting Around

Scooter

Scooters are the most popular way to get around Bali. You can rent them for a low price, and they're a convenient way to explore the island at your own pace.

Bluebird Taxi

Bluebird is a reliable taxi service that operates throughout Bali. You can easily hail a Bluebird taxi on the street or use their mobile app to book a ride.
