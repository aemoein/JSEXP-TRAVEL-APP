Maldives

The Maldives is a tropical paradise located in the Indian Ocean. With pristine white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs, it's the perfect destination for beach lovers and snorkelers. The Maldives is made up of over 1,000 islands, each with its own unique charm and beauty. Visitors can explore local villages, sample delicious Maldivian cuisine, or simply relax in one of the many luxury resorts that dot the islands. Whether you're seeking adventure or relaxation, the Maldives offers an unforgettable experience.

Quick Facts

Capital: Malé
Population: 540,000
Language: Dhivehi
Currency: Maldivian rufiyaa (MVR)
Time Zone: Maldives Time (UTC+05:00)

Climate

The Maldives has a tropical climate with warm temperatures year-round. The average temperature is around 30°C (86°F) and the water temperature is usually between 26°C (79°F) and 29°C (84°F). The Maldives has two main seasons: the dry season from December to April and the wet season from May to November, with the most rain falling from June to August. Regardless of the season, the Maldives is a beautiful destination to visit any time of year.

Top Attractions

Beaches

The Maldives is renowned for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking sunsets. Visitors can relax on the soft white sand, go for a swim, or take a dip in a natural pool formed by the coral reefs.

Snorkeling and Diving

The Maldives boasts some of the world's best snorkeling and diving spots, with abundant marine life and stunning coral reefs. Visitors can explore the underwater world and spot colorful fish, sea turtles, and even sharks.

Island Hopping

With over 1,000 islands, the Maldives offers plenty of opportunities for island hopping. Visitors can discover the unique culture and lifestyle of each island, and enjoy activities like fishing, barbecues, and beach picnics.

Malé

The capital city of Maldives, Malé is a bustling hub of culture and commerce. Visitors can explore the city's historic landmarks, mosques, and markets, and enjoy delicious Maldivian cuisine.

Food & Drink

Maldivian Cuisine

Sample the local cuisine, which is heavily influenced by Indian and Sri Lankan flavors. Don't miss out on dishes like mas huni, garudhiya, and fish curry.

Seafood

As a nation surrounded by the sea, the Maldives is a great place to enjoy fresh seafood. Some popular options include grilled fish, lobster, and shrimp.

Resort Dining

Many resorts in the Maldives offer a range of dining options, from international cuisine to beachside barbecues. Some top choices include Ithaa Undersea Restaurant and Fenesse.

Hotels & Accommodation

Soneva Fushi

This luxurious resort is located on a private island and offers a range of villas and suites, as well as a spa, multiple dining options, and activities like snorkeling and diving.

Conrad Maldives Rangali Island

This resort is known for its overwater villas and underwater restaurant, as well as its beachfront villas and multiple dining options. It also offers a range of activities, including snorkeling, diving, and spa treatments.

Gili Lankanfushi

This eco-friendly resort offers overwater villas, as well as a spa, multiple dining options, and activities like snorkeling, diving, and yoga.

Getting Around

Seaplane

Many resorts in the Maldives are located on private islands, so the best way to get around is by seaplane. Your resort can arrange transportation for you.

Speedboat

If your resort is located closer to the main island, you may be able to take a speedboat to get there. Your resort can provide more information.
